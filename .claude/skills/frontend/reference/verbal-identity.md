# Sunnystep Verbal Identity

## Voice Pillars

### Empowering
Infused with boundless vitality, enthusiasm, and dynamism. Energizes and motivates.

**How to sound empowering:**
- Use action-oriented language: "Seize the opportunity", "Unlock your potential", "Take the next step"
- Affirm the audience's strengths: "Your moves inspire us", "You're unstoppable", "Harness your ambition"
- Provide guidance: "Check out...", "Here are..."

### Positive
Optimistic and sunny disposition. Radiates joy to uplift, inspire, and empower.

**How to sound positive:**
- Maintain an uplifting tone: "We're excited to...", "We're happy to announce...", "We look forward to..."
- Highlight the benefits: "Enhancing", "Empowering", "Transforming"
- Focus on the positive: "Here's how we can help...", "Together, we can...", "We're committed to..."

### Visionary
Forward-thinking, innovative approach to problem-solving, creativity, and transformative change.

**How to sound visionary:**
- Paint a picture: "Pioneering the next wave of...", "Unlocking the potential of...", "Leading the change towards..."
- Incorporate stories of transformation: "Changes lives", "Revolutionizes the industry", "Before and after"

## Key Facts (for accurate copy)

- **Company:** Sunnystep — science-led footwear company
- **Mission:** To empower people to move freely and happily
- **Tagline:** Life is Suntastic
- **Founder:** Mao Ting — started Sunnystep after a postpartum spine injury from yoga. Discovered that the foundation of movement starts with the feet.
- **Science:** Collaboration with MIT Sports Lab on pressure distribution research
- **Differentiators:** Science (MIT-backed insoles), Sustainability (cruelty-free, vegan, algae/recycled materials), Style (modern, unisex, versatile)
- **Insole tech:** Non-sinking polyurethane, deep heel cradle, stable arch support, acupressure massage points, gel cushioning
- **Philosophy:** Joy of Movement — extends beyond products to inspire active, vibrant lifestyles

## Tone Don'ts

- Never clinical or cold — always warm and human
- Never aggressive or pushy — empowering, not pressuring
- Never vague — specific benefits over generic claims
- Never use pain/problem language as the lead — lead with the positive outcome
